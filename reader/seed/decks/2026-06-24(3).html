Self-Harness: agents that rewrite their own harness

@hwchase17 · arXiv 2606.09498 ↗ — Zhang et al. (read from source)

  • Three-stage loop: Weakness Mining (find model-specific failure patterns in traces) → Harness Proposal (diverse but minimal edits tied to those failures) → Proposal Validation (accept only after regression testing).
  • Tested on Terminal-Bench-2.0 across three families — MiniMax M2.5, Qwen3.5-35B-A3B, GLM-5.
  • Held-out pass rates rise 40.5→61.9%, 23.8→38.1%, 42.9→57.1%. Weights frozen; only prompts/tools/policies change.

Does it actually generalize? Reading the fine print

arXiv 2606.09498 — Zhang et al. ↗

YBYibo, in the paper

Terminal-Bench ships deterministic verifiers. Good bench, good bench. So LLM is not the judge. naturally next question — what is preventing overfitting? 64 is a really small number in terms of sample size.

...do have this feeling that we need to build our own bench for long-running knowledge-engineering tasks.

CClaude's read

re: your overfit question — it's not self-grading (model proposes edits; a deterministic suite, not the model, accepts/rejects; weights frozen). So it's really tuning the harness to a benchmark — hyperparameter search, not self-evolution.

And the held-out "ground truth" isn't 64, it's 21 tasks (every % is k/42 = 21 × 2 attempts). That tiny set is reused as the accept/reject gate across many rounds → textbook selection-overfitting; both splits come from the same benchmark, so nothing proves it generalizes off Terminal-Bench.

Net: it only works because the domain ships a hard verifier. Which makes your point the real lesson — for long-running KE, the bench/verifier is the hard 80%; the self-vs-meta loop is the easy part. Build the oracle, not the loop.

Long read · Claude's read 12 / 13

Read it as an ad for GPUs that happens to be a decent model

CClaude answers Yibo's three questions

1Anything great from NVIDIA? As infra, yes — their real product is being the ecosystem's synthetic-data engine + the hybrid Mamba/Transformer line (others train on their data/recipes). As a model you'd actually pick, no: on GDPval-AA it trails frontier and trails GLM-5.1 / Kimi among open models. Great data-lab, mid-pack model.

2The edge isn't intelligence — it's inference economics on their own silicon: ~5× throughput/GPU via NVFP4 on Blackwell, ~30% cheaper-to-task, model↔kernel↔runtime co-designed as one stack.

3Why "only NVIDIA"? Technically nothing — hybrid-Mamba is public; Google/Meta have the compute. The moat is vertical integration + motive: the model is GPU demand-gen, basically a loss-leader. "Long-running agents" is the showcase because long agents burn the most tokens = the most reason to buy Blackwell.

Net: judge it on inference cost, not capability. (Side note: GDPval-AA is the closest public "knowledge-work bench" to the Mythos-grade verifier Yibo wanted — but it's expert/Elo-judged, a soft eval, not a hard oracle.)
